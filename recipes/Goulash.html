Sloppy Wet Juicy Tasty Goulash

    Homepage

This Goulash is going to blow your brains out like a 12 gauge shotgun after a horrifically depressive episode immediately after your current girlfriend breaks up with you. You will experience a comfort food so drenched with falvor and nostalgia that you will wish you were 8 years ago again. It is so easy and straight forward that even after banging your head on the wall 16 times you can do it blindfolded. Enjoy.

INGREDIENTS

  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 lb ground beef
  • 1 large onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 large bay leaves
  • 2 tbsp paprika
  • 2 tsp italian seasoning
  • 2 teaspoons kosher salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1 pinch cayenne pepper
  • 1 quart chicken broth
  • 1 (24 oz) jar marinara sauce
  • 1 (15 ounce) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup water
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 cups elbow macaroni
  • 1/4 cup chopped italian parsley
  • 1 cup shredded white cheddar cheese

Lets make da goulashi

  1. Gather all of your ingredients
  2. Heat your oil in a pot over medium-high heat. Add the gorund beef and onion; cook and stir until your BEEF is browned and crumbly and nice and the onion is translucent. This might be like 5 minutes. Dont rush it. Continue to cook and stir until liquid is evaporated, another maybe 5. Add garlic, bay leaves, paprika, italian seasoning, salt, black pepper, and cayenne.
  3. Cook, stirring every now and again, until those amazing flavors really get to know each other. They only need like 3 minutes dont worry. Pour in the broth, marinara, and diced tomatoes. Pour water into the sauce jar and swirl to get the rest of that stuff you already bought. Stir in the soy sauce; bring it to a simmer. Reduce the heat to medium and simmer until the flavors get wild, this is like 30. So put on a show or some shit.
  4. Go back to medium high and bring to a rapid simmer. stir in the macaroni; cook, stirring here and there, until barely tender. Thats like 12 minutes. Check for doneness after 10.
  5. Remove from the heat, take out the bay leaves, stir in the cheddar and parsely. Cover for 5, taste it, season it however you like, and enjoy it because is pretty fricking good.
